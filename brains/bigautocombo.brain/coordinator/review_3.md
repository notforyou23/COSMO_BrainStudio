1) **goal_6 — Retrofit/conversion market sizing + barriers**

2) **goal_3 — Conversion integration standards + certification pathway**  
   *Rationale:* Compliance is the scalability gate. A clear path through FMVSS/UNECE, state inspection regimes, HV safety, EMC, and documentation requirements directly determines insurability, liability, and ability to partner with fleets/OEM-adjacent channels.

3) **goal_5 — Heavy-duty electrification TCO + infrastructure thresholds**  
   *Rationale:* Heavy-duty is where economics can be most decisive (fuel + maintenance deltas), but only if duty-cycle assumptions and charging constraints are modeled correctly. A duty-cycle TCO engine converts “interest” into “purchase logic.”

4) **goal_4 — Regional adoption sensitivity to policy/incentives**  
   *Rationale:* Policy shapes near-term demand and should drive geographic focus. A scenario model (incentives, ZEV mandates, low-emission zones, electricity pricing) turns a generic market view into a prioritized rollout plan.

5) **goal_2 — Chemistry → BMS/thermal design rules + validation matrix**  
   *Rationale:* Technical credibility + safety. Conversions fail commercially when reliability/warranty risk is unclear. A chemistry-to-design-rule mapping plus a test matrix supports insurance, certification, and reduced field failures.
